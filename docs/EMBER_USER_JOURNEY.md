# EMBER — The User Journey

### Mapped to the HCI canon and Nir Eyal’s second-order principles

-----

## THE TWO LENSES

**The HCI canon.** Norman (affordances, signifiers, feedback, conceptual model, and the Gulfs of Execution & Evaluation), Nielsen’s heuristics (visibility of status, user control, error recovery, minimalism), Fitts’s & Hick’s Laws (target ease, choice load), Jakob’s Law (familiarity), the Aesthetic-Usability effect, and Kahneman’s **Peak-End Rule** (we judge an experience by its peak and its end). Plus **Fogg’s B=MAP** (Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt), the bridge into Eyal.

**Nir Eyal, first and second order.**

- *First order — the Hook Model:* **Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment.**
- *Second order — the ethics:* the **Manipulation Matrix** (axes: *would the maker use it?* × *does it materially improve the user’s life?* → **Facilitator / Entertainer / Peddler / Dealer**; we build only Facilitators) and ***Indistractable*** (everything is either **traction** — toward what the user wants — or **distraction**; the honorable product serves the user’s values, including the value of *not being on it*).

**The governing move:** *we run the Hook Model’s architecture and refuse its dark default.* Every trigger is the user’s own desire to live well; every variable reward resolves into calm rather than escalating; every investment becomes a record that’s genuinely theirs; and the endgame inverts the metric — **success is the user needing Ember less.**

-----

## THE JOURNEY

### PHASE 0 — Arrival & the promise

*From a Substack piece, a friend, or the Commons, to the first open.*

- **Experience:** a single, beautiful frame and one honest sentence — *“a companion that grows from how you actually live.”*
- **HCI:** *Jakob’s Law* — it reads instantly as a care-companion (the Tamagotchi/Finch lineage) so the learning curve is near zero. *Aesthetic-Usability* — the first frame must be gorgeous; beauty buys trust. The *conceptual model* must land in one line (Norman).
- **Eyal (1st):** the **external trigger**, set with an honest expectation — no manufactured FOMO.
- **Eyal (2nd):** *Manipulation Matrix* — Facilitator framing from the first touch; we promise what we’ll deliver.

### PHASE 1 — First session: the aha

*Choose your temperament. Take one breath. Watch a valley light.*

- **Experience:** pick your Ember’s temperament (Seer / Wright / Root / Ferry), name it, and take a single sensed breath that visibly warms the first valley.
- **HCI:** *Hick’s Law* — keep the starter choice tiny (the MVP’s one-or-two temperaments, not twenty). *Progressive disclosure* — reveal the Inner Country slowly; don’t dump the cosmology. *Norman feedback* + *Nielsen #1 (visibility of status)* — the breath must produce *immediate, visible* response, closing the Gulf of Evaluation. *Peak-End* — engineer the first session to **end on the ember catching**, so the stored memory is warmth.
- **Eyal (1st):** the first **Action** is radically easy — *B=MAP* says minimize ability cost, and breathing is the lowest-cost behavior there is. First **Variable Reward** = the world answers a little differently than expected (reward of the *self*: mastery, surprise-of-progress). First **Investment** = naming + choosing, which personalizes and loads the next visit.
- **Eyal (2nd):** the aha is *recognition* — the user seeing their own state reflected — not a dopamine spike. *Indistractable:* the very first loop is traction (toward their own well-being), establishing the right internal trigger from minute one.

### PHASE 2 — The daily loop

*Live; the Witness notices; the country answers.*

- **Experience:** a calm daily presence. You don’t tap to feed — your breath, rest, movement, attention, and reaching-for-others are sensed, and the Inner Country reflects them.
- **HCI:** *Visibility of system status* shown as a living world, **not** a Whoop-style 0–100 verdict — minimalist, calm, never a metrics dashboard. *Peak-End per session* — every visit ends on warmth. *Goal-gradient* used gently (a region nearing bloom invites, never nags).
- **Eyal (1st):** **Trigger** matures from external to **internal** — but the internal trigger we cultivate is *the felt desire to tend / curiosity about your Ember*, not anxiety. **Variable Reward** = what the day’s living produced (the Hearthkeeper’s overnight dream-harvest hands you a new idea at dawn — variable, but bounded). **Investment** = waking the Circle, the accreting world.
- **Eyal (2nd):** the internal trigger is bound to the *user’s own value* (living well), never to manufactured discomfort. *Indistractable:* the loop is traction, not distraction. The reward **resolves into calm** instead of escalating into a slot machine — the structural refusal of the Dealer quadrant.

### PHASE 3 — The lapse, the Hush, and the return  ⭐ *the differentiator*

*Where every competitor punishes, we welcome.*

- **Experience:** you drift. The country greys (the Hush): color drains, recent tools sleep, storms land harder. But the ember holds. When you return, the first moment is **relief and welcome** — never a debt screen.
- **HCI:** there is *no error state to recover from* — lapsing is reframed out of “failure” entirely. The Hush is honest *system status* without judgment (*Nielsen: error prevention* becomes *blame prevention*). *Peak-End applied to the return* — the re-entry moment is deliberately a peak of warmth, the inverse of the loss-aversion debt screen.
- **Eyal (1st):** this is exactly where the naive Hook Model reaches for **variable punishment / loss aversion / streak anxiety** to drag you back.
- **Eyal (2nd):** we refuse it on principle. *Manipulation Matrix:* weaponizing guilt here is the **Dealer** move; the ember-never-dies is the *structural guarantee* we cannot fall into it. *Indistractable:* Eyal’s own later warning is that the internal trigger of discomfort drives compulsion — so we never manufacture the discomfort. **This single design choice is the most legible proof that Ember is a Facilitator.**

### PHASE 4 — Deepening: the Circle, the Seasons, mastery

*Investment becomes a life recorded.*

- **Experience:** over weeks the world unfolds; you draft and rotate your Circle; you survive your first storm and feel the country *hold* where it once shattered.
- **HCI:** *Progressive disclosure* across weeks; *flow* (challenge matched to growing skill); the *conceptual model* deepens; *consistency*. Surviving a storm is a designed **peak**.
- **Eyal (1st):** **Investment** is the engine — stored value (your grown country, your woken Circle, your season-history) makes Ember more valuable the more you live, and loads richer future triggers.
- **Eyal (2nd):** the stored value is *genuinely the user’s* — a true record of how they’ve lived, not lock-in for its own sake. Facilitator, sustained.

### PHASE 5 — The Commons: kinship, not comparison

*The most powerful reward type — handled with the most care.*

- **Experience:** you tend alongside others; you can send warmth, co-regulate, share a season — never compete.
- **HCI:** *social proof* and familiar social patterns (*Jakob*), but designed for **encouragement, not ranking** (Finch’s “friends, not competition” is the right prior art). No vanity metrics.
- **Eyal (1st):** the **Variable Reward of the Tribe** — the strongest and most dangerous reward.
- **Eyal (2nd):** the tribe reward must be **co-regulatory (the Kin), not status-competitive** — leaderboards manufacture envy (the literal *Inside Out* failure mode) and would betray the whole thesis. *Manipulation Matrix:* the Commons must help, or it doesn’t ship.

### PHASE 6 — The inversion: the Indistractable endgame

*The success metric most apps would never accept.*

- **Experience:** the best sessions become the ones where Ember effectively says *“go live — I’ll be here,”* and the country thrives **because** you put the phone down and went and breathed, trained, and connected for real.
- **HCI:** this is the *conceptual model* fully realized — the app’s job was always to point you back at your life. It is anti-pattern to standard engagement design, and intentionally so.
- **Eyal (2nd):** the explicit **Facilitator endgame**. *Indistractable* made literal: Ember is a tool for **traction toward the user’s own well-being**, and it dissolves into the background as the practice internalizes. The **regret test** is passed — on reflection, the user is glad. Our north-star metric is *lives tended*, never *minutes captured*.

## WHAT TO PROTOTYPE & MEASURE

- **The aha (Phase 1):** time-to-first-lit-valley. If the first breath doesn’t visibly warm the world inside ~20 seconds, the Gulf of Evaluation is too wide.
- **The return (Phase 3):** the make-or-break test. Prototype the re-entry-after-a-lapse screen first; if a returning user feels *relief* and not *debt*, the whole thesis holds. (This is the screen that proves we’re a Facilitator.)
- **The metric:** instrument **“days the user lived well off-app”** alongside in-app time — and be willing to celebrate when off-app time goes *up*. If we can’t stomach that metric, we’ve quietly become a Dealer.

-----

## OPEN DECISIONS TO DIRECT

1. **The internal trigger we cultivate** — curiosity (“what did my living make today?”) vs. care (“I want to tend it”) vs. relief (“this is where I exhale”). They produce different products. My lean: *care*, with relief as the felt texture.
1. **Notifications** — do we send external triggers at all? *Indistractable* would say: only ones the user scheduled for themselves (traction prompts), never ones we time for our metrics.
1. **Where the inversion shows up** — is “go live, I’ll be here” an explicit feature, or an emergent property we never name? Naming it is bolder and riskier.
