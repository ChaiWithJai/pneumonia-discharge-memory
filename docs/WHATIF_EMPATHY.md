# What-If Empathy Layer

The what-if layer is designed to make discharge risk legible without turning patient suffering into spectacle.

## Purpose

For each patient, the runtime can emit image prompts for safe, respectful scenario visualization:

- Discharge today with standard instructions.
- Delay 24 hours and reassess.
- Discharge with medication and home support.

## Guardrails

- No identifiable patient likeness.
- No real chart screenshots.
- No gore.
- No fear tactics.
- No claim that image output is evidence.
- No autonomous clinical recommendation.

## Why It Matters

Discharge failures are often operational and human:

- Medication access.
- Caregiver availability.
- Health literacy.
- Mobility decline.
- Follow-up friction.

Images can help teams empathize with the lived environment of discharge, but the clinical reasoning must remain in the trace.
